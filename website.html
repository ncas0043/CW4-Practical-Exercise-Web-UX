What is Typography?

Two typographic posters made in DGA 1019

Typography is the art of making use of different typefaces and trying out different combinations of size, fonts and spacing. Typography is seen on everything nowadays, including posters, brochures, adverts, art prints, books, website design and a lot more. Graphic designers usually combine images and text to make typographic posters, Typography lets graphic designers create artwork and often have to make crucial decisions over attributes like font, size, spacing, placement and other things.

Why is Typography important?

Typography is important because it is a way to convey emotions through text, not only that but it also grabs the viewer's attention when it comes to advertisements, logos, consumer products and web design, it also helps build brand recognition as well as establishing value.

Typeface vs Font

A typeface is the name of a particular group of fonts, whereas a font refers to the specific type of weight, style and width within that typeface. To simplify, each form of a typeface is a font. For reference, Arial could be the chosen typeface for a typography graphicbut the font one might be using is an Arial bold italic 12 points.
